The Delight of Falafel

Introduction to Falafel

Falafel is a popular Middle Eastern food made from ground chickpeas or fava beans, formed into balls or patties, and deep-fried to a crispy perfection. It is often served in pita bread or flatbread and accompanied by salads, pickles, and tahini or hummus.

History of Falafel

The exact origin of falafel is unclear, but it is believed to have originated in Egypt and later spread across the Middle East. It has become a staple street food and is now enjoyed worldwide, particularly by those looking for a flavorful vegetarian option.

Why People Love Falafel

  • Rich in flavor with a crispy outside and soft inside.
  • A healthy source of protein, especially for vegetarians and vegans.
  • Versatile: can be eaten as a snack, in a sandwich, or as part of a full meal.
  • Commonly enjoyed with a variety of toppings and sauces, such as hummus, tahini, or yogurt sauce.

How to Make Falafel

To make falafel, chickpeas (or fava beans) are soaked, then ground and mixed with herbs, garlic, and spices. The mixture is shaped into balls or patties, then deep-fried or baked. It can be served as part of a meal or eaten as a quick snack.
